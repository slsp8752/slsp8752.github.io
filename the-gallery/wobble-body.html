OVER&OVER&OVER&OVER
A digital collage piece using old travel photos, a circuit bent camera, and a dinosaur wire-frame.
The background texture is made from various photos with rough features, all mushed together with a threshold filter.
The glitchy colors come from a circuit bent toy camera. I intended to add controls to the camera, but settled for a sloppy solder blob on the sensor pins.
Low poly dinosaur fed into plotter.vision
(You should play with all the tools on drawingbots.com )
I tend to make art without a lot of emotional intent in the moment. I usually figure out how I was feeling or what the art means to me after the fact.
I added in the subtle "OVER&OVER&OVER" text after feeling the constant barrage of just trying to exist in 2026 weighing down on me. The image as a whole feels like trying to persist in a sea of NOISE.
Overlaid "ghosting" effect made by taking a picture of my computer screen, then taking a picture of that picture, repeated 4 times.
I didn't notice the mouse cursor in the final result until I was making this slide!
Another digital collage featuring a dinosaur! I may have a fondness for them...
Lucky Star
This one is made up of old non-digital art that I scanned and blended together. In total the elements span a bit over 10 years!
It was fun revisiting some old doodles that would have otherwise withered away in 3 separate sketchbooks.
I remember being inspired by Kristoffer Zetterstrand's art when I made this. He made paintings that ended up in Minecraft, which had just entered beta at the time.
This piece let me indulge in some nostalgia and see how my old styles and techniques mesh with the nonsense I'm doing now. It feels warm and whimsical.
The background texture in front of the bigger star was created using Lunar Lab's "Dither Machine". I took a small section of black and white dither and scaled it up a bunch.
One big ol' mutant fish featuring scanned stamps. I really enjoy the variety of texture of this piece.
Swimming Upstream
The reason the "fish" looks so bizarre is that it started its life as a bird! I layered and connected the wings of this bird that I found in a birdwatching book, then drew on top of it.
I regret cutting off the right side of the weird splashy tongue.
The lighthouses come from a piece of promotional mail from the United States Postal Service. The promo contained previews of stamps from the 2021 "Mid-Atlantic Lighthouses" collection.
This is a block print I received at a local Dios de Los Muertos event.
I decided to scan these because I had been holding on to them for too long, hoping that I would make something out of them. I eventually found I was hoarding too many paper scraps, so I scanned them and threw the physical scraps out. The act of scanning them made me more inspired to use them.
Some of the background is composed of kaleidoscope-d bread tabs. This bread tab is classified as "Cyrtotergum albus" by "The Holotypic Occlupanid Research Group"
More old art! I was (am) obsessed with overlapping geometric noise.

Sixth Sense
I was inspired by wormyorchid/Jam's "Postage" zine series, which is why the bird is on a stamp. Also because it gave me the chance to make a bad pun.
I was also inspired by Pablo Delcan's cover for the book "Annihilation". The book's contents also inspired me, but the cover hooked me first.
"Sixth Sense" is actually the third piece in a series of experiments that came from my Annihilation-sparked inspiration. These other two served nicely as stepping stones to all of the other pieces in the gallery.
The bird that grew extra eyes in this piece is a Sprague's Pipit. Very cute songbird. Specifically, this illustration is by Dale A. Zimmerman
The background swooshes come from this image from the Biodiversity Heritage Library. Specifically from Banshō zukan by "Kobayashi, Bunshichi", circa 1901 Japan.
"The Gallery" was created as a submission for "Torn Leaf", and experimental online gallery.
I thought submitting an art gallery to an art gallery was a funny idea.
The gallery you're exploring was made with wareware. wareware is a quirky game engine currently in development by riprip. What it lacks in conditional logic and tile rotation, it makes up tenfold in charm.
All of the gallery content was created using wwwobble. wwwobble is a really fun way to quickly smash together fun looking webpages.
